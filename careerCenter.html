CAREER CENTER

OVERVIEW

While most students would list getting a good, professional-level job as a major objective of getting a college education, a large percentage of students do not utilize most or all of the services provided to them by their placement office/career center. Why this disconnect? Is it lack of information, fear, perceptions or misperceptions or something else? Do they simply not feel the need? Who better to figure this out and attract students to the programs of the Career Center than current MSU ad/promo students?

OBJECTIVES

1. Increase awareness of the department and all that it offers to current students, alumni and “friends/employers” in such a way that it can become a model for other Career Centers throughout the nation by strengthening the “brand.”
2. Prioritize your various target markets and determine what they currently know about this organization in terms of top of mind awareness and what they need to know.
3. Develop unique promotional strategies—utilizing some traditional but mainly non-traditional forms of promotion--that target these young adults/target markets and make the Career Center stand out among its competitors—both on and off campus
4. Develop an event to showcase the Career Center or new ways to showcase existing events that would appeal to the young adult/college market who might be considering using the offered resources
5. Make current and potential MSU students and alumni aware of the opportunities available to them through the Career Center and encourage students to “come on board.”
